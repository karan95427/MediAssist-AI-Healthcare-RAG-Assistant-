Cancer is a group of diseases in which abnormal cells grow and divide in an uncontrolled way. These cells can sometimes invade nearby tissue or spread to other parts of the body. Cancer is not one single condition; it includes many different diseases, such as breast cancer, lung cancer, colorectal cancer, blood cancers, and others.

Common warning signs can include an unexplained lump, unexplained weight loss, persistent fatigue, unusual bleeding, a sore that does not heal, ongoing changes in bowel or bladder habits, persistent cough, or new changes in a mole. These symptoms can also happen for reasons that are not cancer, so medical evaluation is important.

Diagnosis usually depends on clinical assessment, imaging, lab tests, and sometimes biopsy. Treatment varies by cancer type, stage, overall health, and patient goals, and may include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, hormone therapy, or a combination of approaches.

People should seek advice from a qualified healthcare professional if they notice persistent or unexplained symptoms, have abnormal screening results, or have concerns about cancer risk.
